Scratchboard Etchings

These things don't translate well to digital forms, but they're cool and shiny and catch the light like a 3D object would.

The Process

The physical scratchboard is a metallic clay board covered in a coat of India ink. First, the design is outlined on the board with a fine layer of chalk. Portions of the ink coating are then selectively etched away with a blade to reveal the surface below. The subtractive process feels foreign throughout, but outputs a relatively photorealistic result.

Here's a work in progress. It's tricky to notice when viewed on a screen, but both the beak and eye are outlined in chalk dust, rather than having been scratched away. Once the thing is finished, all remaining chalk powder is wiped away with the (now) powdered ink.
